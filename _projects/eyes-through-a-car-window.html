Though {{ page.title }} came together with very little planning, it would be the first of several projects involving a combination of my own drawing and computer programming. I consider Narrow Focus the last of this series. Thanks to Amy Podmore for helping me find my limits on this project.

Each print is the result of a Processing sketch iterating on one of twelve ink drawings. Step by step, a section of the drawing is cut out and rendered back onto itself. After thousands of iterations, this produces a textured black rectangle that retains some of the qualities of the original drawing. The drawings themselves are inspired by a collection of early 20th century photographs held by the Library of Congress.
